Step 1: Choose Your LLC Name

Your LLC’s name is the foundation of your brand. It must be unique and comply with your state’s rules. Avoid names too similar to existing businesses and check availability through your state’s business name database. Don’t forget to consider your website domain name too.

Step 2: Appoint a Registered Agent

Every LLC needs a registered agent to receive legal notices and government correspondence. While you can technically serve as your own, most business owners prefer professionals for reliability and privacy.

Step 3: File Your Articles of Organization

This is the official paperwork that establishes your LLC with the state. Filing fees vary depending on where you register. Once approved, your LLC legally exists as a business entity.

Step 4: Create an Operating Agreement

An operating agreement outlines how your LLC will run, including member roles, voting rights, and profit distribution. Even if not legally required in your state, it’s a smart move for preventing disputes.

Step 5: Get an EIN (Employer Identification Number)

An EIN from the IRS is like a Social Security number for your business. You’ll need it to open a business bank account, hire employees, and handle taxes. You can apply for free directly on the IRS website.

Step 6: Comply with State & Local Requirements

Depending on your industry, you may need licenses or permits. Check with your local city and county offices to make sure your business meets all requirements.
